Lehmer's method is a type of "Linear Congruential Method" which operates in a multiplicative group of integers modulo $m$.


The recursive formula applied in the method is:


$$x_{n+1} = ax_n \mod m.$$

There are many ways of choosing the numbers $a$, $m$ and the seed $x_0$. Different values would cause the method to reach its period faster when such numbers are not "good enough".

Choosing $a, m$ and $x_0$

In general we want our choice of $m$ to be the highest possible prime number, this comes, however, at a big computing cost. For this reason, it's very common to work with numbers $m$ that are a power of $2$. Doing so guarantees us that the period would be at most $m/4$, which is pretty low, but this period would be even smaller when $m$ is even. Due to this, a good option for $m$ is, for example $m = 2^{32}+1 = 4294967297$.


The best choice for $x_0$ is to make it coprime with $m$, this becomes a trivial task when $m$ is prime and a bit more complicaten when it is not.

Suggested values: $a = $16843009, $m =$ 4294967297
